# Marketing Plan — Digital Sun

> Agent: Marketing (Go-to-Market & Growth)
> Created: 2026-03-07
> Status: Draft — awaiting review

---

## Current State Assessment

- **Zero public presence**: No website, no social accounts, no press, no waitlist
- **No product photography**: No physical product exists yet to photograph
- **No copy written**: No product description, no artist statement, no press pitch
- **Target audience defined in theory**: Design-forward, 28-45, Are.na-adjacent — but untested
- **Positioning drafted**: "A lamp that contains a living painting" — not a smart light
- **No competitive analysis done**: Benchmarks listed (Nanoleaf, TE, prints) but no deep comparison
- **Channel options identified**: Instagram, Kickstarter, design press, galleries — none activated

## Goal

Build a small, engaged pre-launch audience and prepare all materials needed to sell the first 10 units through one channel.

## Plan

### 1. Start a Build Log (Immediately)

Document the build process publicly. This can start before the product exists.

- **Deliverable**: Instagram account or personal blog with 8-12 posts showing: LED strips, soldering, first light-up, diffuser tests, shader screenshots
- **Success Criteria**: Content exists and is publicly accessible; at least 3 posts before the first prototype is complete
- **Dependencies**: None — use what exists now (shader screenshots from OPENRNDR, hardware spec diagrams, strip photos)
- **Priority**: Critical — the only task that can start today

**Content themes**:
- Process shots: strips being cut, soldered, mounted
- Shader development: OPENRNDR screenshots with brief captions about the math
- Design decisions: "why diamond?", "choosing a diffuser"
- Ambient video: 15-second clips of the shader running (even on screen)

### 2. Capture Product Video

One 30-60 second video of the finished prototype in a dark room. This is the primary sales asset.

- **Deliverable**: 1 hero video (landscape, 1080p minimum) + 3 vertical cuts for social
- **Success Criteria**: Video shows the light behavior clearly; conveys warmth and slowness; no visible UI or hands; could be posted with zero caption and still communicate the product
- **Dependencies**: Physical prototype complete with diffuser (`electronics-engineer` + `designer`)
- **Priority**: Critical — this is the product demo

**Production notes**:
- Shoot on a tripod in a genuinely dark room (not dimmed — dark)
- Include 5 seconds of the lamp off (show the object), then let it breathe
- No music. Ambient room sound or silence.
- Shoot at multiple distances: close-up (texture), mid (object on wall), wide (room context)

### 3. Build a Simple Product Page

One page where someone can understand and buy the product.

- **Deliverable**: Single-page website or a well-crafted shop listing (Etsy, Big Cartel, or Shopify single-product)
- **Success Criteria**: Page loads fast, shows the video, has the product description, states the price, has a buy/pre-order button. Works on mobile.
- **Dependencies**: Video (Task 2); copy from `writer`; price from `pricing`
- **Priority**: High — this is where purchases happen

### 4. Seed to Tastemakers

Get the product in front of 10-20 specific people who influence the target audience.

- **Deliverable**: List of 20 target accounts/people + personalized outreach message
- **Success Criteria**: At least 5 meaningful responses (DM reply, repost, or expressed interest); not mass outreach — each message is specific to the person
- **Dependencies**: Product page live (Task 3); video exists (Task 2)
- **Priority**: Medium — amplification after the foundation is set

**Target profiles**:
- Independent interior designers who post their own spaces
- Generative art practitioners (Processing/p5.js community)
- Design object reviewers on YouTube (small channels, 5K-50K subscribers)
- Local gallery curators in Lithuania/Baltics

### 5. Decide: DTC vs. Kickstarter

Make the channel decision with data, not theory.

- **Deliverable**: Channel recommendation with pros/cons based on audience response from Tasks 1-4
- **Success Criteria**: Decision is made and justified; launch prep begins within 1 week of decision
- **Dependencies**: Pre-launch audience feedback (Tasks 1, 4); pricing confirmed (`pricing`)
- **Priority**: Medium — this is a gate before scaling, but first 1-3 sales can happen DTC regardless

## Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Build log gets no engagement | High | Low | Engagement isn't the goal — content library is. Keep posting regardless. |
| Video doesn't capture the feel of the light | Medium | High | Shoot extensively; test different cameras (phone vs. mirrorless); LEDs on camera are tricky — may need manual exposure |
| Tastemaker outreach feels spammy | Medium | Medium | Keep it personal and brief; lead with the work, not a pitch; accept that most won't respond |
| Kickstarter campaign fails to fund | Medium | High | Set conservative goal (10-20 units); only launch after pre-launch signals are positive |
